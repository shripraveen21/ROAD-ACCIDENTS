More than half of people involved in road accidents had drugs or alcohol in their system, study says

by Sharmaji

Every day in the US, 29 people die in car crashes that involve an alcohol-impaired driver, according to the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and it's unclear how many more are killed in accidents involving drivers under the influence of drugs.However, the researchers of the new study sought to expand what's known about road safety by looking beyond drivers. Their study, published this week by the US National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, looked at information on passengers, bikers, electric scooter riders and pedestrians who were involved in accidents, as well as drivers.About 11% of people tested positive for some form of stimulant, like cocaine or methamphetamines, and 9% had opioids in their system.In 32% of the fatal accidents and 18% of the ones with serious injuries, there were two or more drugs in the system of someone involved.

Indian Man Fined Rs 5.5 Lakhs For Drunk Driving

The cases seemed to grow

The penalty for drunk-driving and driving under the influence includes imprisonment and a possible fine of at least 20,000 dirhams, including confiscation of the vehicle for 60 days.

A 39 year-old Indian man has been fined 25,000 dirhams (Rs 5,56,672) for drunken-driving and absconding from the accident scene after crashing his vehicle into a car in Dubai. The man was reportedly driving through a neighbourhood in Bur Dubai when he lost control of the car and swerved into another vehicle that was parked on the right side of the road.

The incident happened on August 18 and even though the man’s name was withheld in the report, he was arrested within a few hours by the police after cameras identified the car number plate. Later, he confessed to the police that he was driving his car under the influence of alcohol, following which he spent two days in custody before being released on bail.

Drunk Mahindra Thar driver rams into several cars

A thousand of wounds and hundred of lives

In a recent disturbing example of a hit-and-run, a Mahindra Thar owner was arrested by Patiala police for allegedly hitting other vehicles on the roads of Patiala. The Thar driver was said to be under the influence of alcohol, though the reason behind such action by him is still unknown. A video showing the Mahindra Thar driver ramming into parked vehicles on the roadside has gone viral on social media platforms.However, despite this, the Mahindra Thar driver refused to stop and in an attempt to flee from the spot, he started driving the SUV even more rashly. However, after several attempts, the locals at the spot managed to stop the Thar and nab the driver along with two other youths accompanying him in the Thar. All three men, identified as Amit, Parminder and Deepinder, are aged under 25 and were said to be under the influence of alcohol. After confronting them, the locals handed all three of them to the Patiala police.

Drink and Drug Drivig 2022

by FRANZ KAFKA

Since the launch of the campaign officers in North Yorkshire have been out patrolling the county in marked and unmarked vehicles. A specialist operation was also set up on the A66 corridor. Officers from North Yorkshire Police partnered with officers from Cumbria Police, Durham Police and the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) to target commercial drivers who use drink or drugs. A number of drivers were dealt with for motoring offences but thankfully all drivers were negative for drink or drugs.

It's not just about having a drink or taking drugs and getting straight into the car – you can also be over the limit the morning after. As part of the campaign officers have also been conducting static checks early in the morning.The campaign this year focuses on people calling out behaviour. Members of the public are being asked to “save a life and call it in.” If you suspect that a driver is behind the wheel when under the influence of drink or drugs, please call 999.

Road Accidents

Road accidents are a major cause of injury and death worldwide. They can occur due to a variety of factors, including reckless or negligent driving, alcohol or drug impairment, distracted driving, and poor road conditions. Road accidents can have serious consequences for those involved, including physical injury, property damage, and financial burdens. It is important for all road users to take precautions and practice safe driving behaviors to reduce the risk of accidents.
